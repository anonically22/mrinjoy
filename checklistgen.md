# IPR Firm Website Checklist
---

## Required Pages

- Home.
- About Us.
- Services / Expertise.
- Individual service detail pages for each core practice area.
- FAQ.
- Clients / Industries Served.
- Contact Us.
- Legal pages:
  - Disclaimer.
  - Privacy Policy.
  - Terms and Conditions.
  - IPR / Copyright notice.
- Location pages, if the firm has multiple offices.

---

## Home Page Must Include

- Firm name and legal identity.
- Clear value proposition.
- Hero section with at least one primary CTA and one secondary CTA.
- Short introduction to the firm.
- Summary of core services.
- Trust signals such as experience, specialization, or client types.
- Quick links to key pages.
- Footer with contact details and legal links.

---

## About Page Must Include

- Firm overview.
- Mission or philosophy.
- What the firm is.
- What the firm does.
- Why the firm is different.
- Team or leadership overview, if applicable.
- Optional history, timeline, or milestones.
- Optional service coverage or office locations.

---

## Services Landing Page Must Include

- Intro to the firm’s practice areas.
- A visible list, grid, or cards for all services.
- Each service item should include:
  - Service name.
  - Short description.
  - Link to the full service page.
- Optional grouping by category:
  - IP registration.
  - IP prosecution.
  - Litigation / enforcement.
  - Corporate / advisory.
  - Tax / compliance.
  - International / cross-border support.

---

## Core Service Pages

The site should have separate pages for the key IPR/legal services the firm offers, such as:

- Trademark.
- Patent.
- Design.
- Copyright.
- IP Litigation / Enforcement.
- Corporate Law.
- Taxation Advisory.
- Global / International IP support.

---

## Service Detail Page Template

Each service page should contain:

- Service title.
- Service summary.
- Who the service is for.
- Problems it solves.
- What is included.
- Workflow or process.
- Deliverables.
- Timeline expectations.
- Required documents or inputs.
- CTA to contact or book consultation.
- Related service links.
- Service-specific FAQ snippets.

---

## FAQ Page Must Include

- General service questions.
- Filing / registration process questions.
- Timeline questions.
- Document requirement questions.
- Cost / pricing explanation.
- Jurisdiction / international filing questions.
- Enforcement / opposition / objection questions.
- Clear contact path for unanswered questions.

---

## Clients Page Must Include

- Client logos or client names, if permitted.
- Industry categories served.
- Short trust statement.
- Optional case study snippets.
- Optional geographic coverage or market focus.

---

## Contact Page Must Include

- Office address.
- Phone number.
- Email address.
- Business hours.
- Contact form.
- Map embed or location link.
- Social links, if used.
- Clear call to action.
- Separate sections for each office, if multiple offices exist.

---

## Legal Pages Must Include

- Disclaimer.
- Privacy Policy.
- Terms and Conditions.
- IPR / Copyright notice.
- Confirmation / consent notice, if required.
- Footer links to all legal pages from every page.

---

## Sitewide Must-Have Elements

- Consistent header navigation.
- Mobile-responsive menu.
- Footer with full contact details.
- CTA buttons on important pages.
- Internal links between Home, Services, FAQ, and Contact.
- SEO basics:
  - Title tag.
  - Meta description.
  - H1 on every page.
  - Image alt text.
- Accessibility basics:
  - Readable contrast.
  - Keyboard navigation.
  - Form labels.
- Structured data where relevant.
- Fast-loading assets.
- Clear location and jurisdiction wording.

---

## Exclusions

Do **not** require these for this checklist:

- Testimonials.
- Awards / recognition.
- Careers / jobs.

---

## Validation Checklist for Coding Agent

Use this instruction for automated checking:

- Confirm all required pages exist.
- Confirm each page contains its mandatory blocks.
- Flag missing navigation links.
- Flag missing footer links.
- Flag missing CTA buttons.
- Flag missing legal pages.
- Flag missing service detail pages.
- Flag pages that mix outdated, irrelevant, or unrelated copy.
- Flag pages that do not provide a clear next step for the user.
- Mark each item as:
  - Present.
  - Missing.
  - Incomplete.

## Notes for Your Build

- Keep the navigation simple and consistent.
- Make every service page self-contained.
- Ensure the legal pages are easy to find from the footer.
- Remove unrelated or outdated copy before launch.
- Make the firm’s geography clear everywhere on the site.
